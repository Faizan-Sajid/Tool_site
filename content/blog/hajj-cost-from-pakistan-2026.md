## Government Hajj Package vs Private — Which One Is Right for You?

| Factor | Government Package | Private Package |
|---|---|---|
| Price | Lower — subsidized | Higher — market rate |
| Hotel distance | Often 1–2 km+ from Haram | Often closer |
| Selection method | Computerized ballot | First come, first served |
| Group size | Very large batches | Smaller groups |
| Flexibility | Fixed dates, fixed itinerary | More options available |
| Meals | Usually included | Varies by package |

In Pakistan, the government Hajj scheme is managed by the **Ministry of Religious Affairs**. Applications open in January each year. Because demand always exceeds supply, a computerized ballot selects pilgrims. Applying in January gives you the best chance.

If you are not selected in the government ballot, private operators offer packages year-round. They cost more but tend to have better hotel locations and smaller groups.

**The Pakistan government Hajj package is cheaper than private packages by 20–30%.** However, it uses a ballot system due to high demand. Private packages start at PKR 1,375,000 in 2026. Apply through [mora.gov.pk](https://www.mora.gov.pk) for the government scheme.

## What Is Included in a Hajj Package from Pakistan?

Most pilgrims get surprised after booking. The headline price rarely covers everything.

| Cost Item | Usually Included? | If Extra — Estimated Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Return flights | Depends on package | PKR 80,000 – 150,000 extra |
| Makkah hotel | Yes | Distance and stars vary |
| Madinah hotel | Yes — 3 to 5 nights | Duration varies |
| Mina tent | Yes | Comfort level varies |
| Arafat / Muzdalifah transport | Yes | Quality varies |
| Visa fee (SAR 300) | Usually included | Approx PKR 22,300 |
| Meals | Varies | Not always full board |
| Qurbani sacrifice | Sometimes | SAR 350–600 = PKR 35,000–60,000 |
| Ihram clothing | No | Around PKR 2,000–3,000 |
| Personal expenses | No | Budget PKR 30,000–60,000 extra |
| Travel insurance | Sometimes | Verify with operator |

One thing many pilgrims miss: **shifting vs non-shifting packages**. A shifting package moves you from a Mina-area hotel to a Makkah hotel after the peak Hajj days. It costs less. A non-shifting package keeps you in one hotel throughout — easier for elderly pilgrims or anyone with mobility concerns.

Four practical tips:

1. Open a **dedicated savings account** labelled "Hajj Fund" — keeping it separate makes you less likely to spend it.
2. Use an **Islamic bank profit-sharing account** — Meezan Bank, Bank Islami, and Al Baraka all offer halal savings options.
3. **Book early** — packages reserved 12+ months ahead are consistently cheaper than last-minute bookings.
4. **Watch the SAR/PKR rate** — when PKR strengthens, your SAR-based costs effectively drop. Time your final payment accordingly.

**To save for Hajj from Pakistan, set aside PKR 25,000 per month to afford an economy package in 5 years.** For a standard package, save PKR 36,700 per month. Book early — packages reserved 12+ months in advance cost consistently less.
